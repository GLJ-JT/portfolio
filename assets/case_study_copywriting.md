Worldschooling

a streamlined digital platform for an educational travel package suppliers to showcase programs and drive package upgrades.
00

problem
Worldschooling, an educational travel startup competing in the space pioneered by companies like EF Education First, faced a critical challenge. They needed a sophisticated website to showcase their three-tier product range and guide customers toward premium packages, but traditional educational travel websites are notoriously complex and slow to build. Their planned architecture included campus pages, subject pages, program pages, a booking portal, blogs, and a product search system—a scope that would delay their market entry and fundraising timeline at a moment when speed was essential.

solution
The solution was to strategically simplify the user journey without sacrificing conversion potential. The booking portal was replaced with a streamlined flow: home page to product filter for selection, product detail pages for exploration, an enrollment form to gather information and check eligibility, and finally a universal deposit payment link. This architectural decision shortened the development cycle dramatically while maintaining a premium feel. Strategic UX interventions drove upsells: an interactive category selector on the homepage encouraged exploration of mid-tier products, while playful animations on entry-level program pages nudged users toward premium offerings.

Worldschooling's founders understood that in the educational travel industry, timing is everything. They had a compelling vision—empowering students through unique, transformative experiences around the world—but they needed a digital platform that could match the sophistication of established players while launching in a fraction of the time. The challenge wasn't just building a website; it was building the right website, one that could grow with the company and drive the business metrics that matter most: engagement, conversion, and package upgrades.
The design process began with a critical decision: what could be simplified without compromising the user experience? Traditional educational travel sites are built around complex booking systems that take months to develop and integrate. Instead, the team designed a user flow that felt seamless but was technically straightforward. Students and parents would discover programs through an intuitive filter system, dive deep into program details on dedicated pages, complete an enrollment form that doubled as an eligibility check, and then move to a simple payment link for their deposit. This approach eliminated the need for a custom booking portal while actually improving clarity and reducing friction.
The real innovation came in the upsell strategy. On the homepage, showcasing mid-tier programs through an interactive category selector transformed passive browsing into active exploration. Users weren't just scrolling past options—they were clicking, engaging, and discovering programs tailored to their interests. For students viewing entry-level programs, subtle but delightful animations drew attention to premium program recommendation cards, creating moments of curiosity that encouraged users to explore higher-value offerings. Every interaction was designed to feel natural, never pushy, but always guiding users toward the experiences that would serve them best.
The results validated every decision. The founder was thrilled with the platform's polish and performance. Early user testing revealed high engagement rates and strong Net Promoter Scores, signaling that the experience resonated with both students and parents. Most importantly, the accelerated timeline meant Worldschooling could launch with confidence and enter their fundraising phase with a digital presence that demonstrated both their vision and their execution capabilities. The platform wasn't just a website—it was proof that the company could deliver on its promise.

Travel to Qin

an immersive digital platform for a Chinese inbound travel agency to inspire and convert foreign visitors.
00

problem
Foreign travelers seeking to visit China faced a significant barrier when exploring Travel to Qin's services. Despite being backed by the prestigious China International Travel Service and positioned to capitalize on the September 2024 visa policy ease, their digital presence was a basic Shopify checkout page. The site lacked any compelling narrative to showcase the rich cultural experiences of traveling through China, failed to inspire potential visitors, and wasn't optimized for social media platforms like Instagram and TikTok where modern travelers discover and share their journeys.

solution
Travel to Qin received a complete digital transformation, from brand identity to technical infrastructure. The new platform features an immersive, video-first landing experience that immediately captures the magic and scale of China. A narrative-driven information architecture guides visitors through curated stories and stunning visuals, while a headless CMS empowers the team to manage content independently. The scalable design system proved so successful it was extended to their mobile app, creating a cohesive brand experience that converts browsers into travelers.

Travel to Qin's transformation began at a pivotal moment. When China relaxed its visa policies in September 2024, opening doors to millions of potential visitors, the agency found itself with a golden opportunity but a digital storefront that couldn't tell their story. The founders recognized that in an era where travelers seek authentic, immersive experiences, their generic e-commerce site was actively working against them. They needed a digital presence that could compete with the polished, narrative-driven platforms travelers had come to expect.

The project started with a fundamental reimagining of the brand. Drawing inspiration from traditional Chinese calligraphy and landscape painting, a new visual identity emerged that felt both timeless and contemporary. The color palette blended deep teals and warm golds with soft neutrals, creating a sophisticated aesthetic that would appeal to an international audience while honoring Chinese heritage. This brand foundation became the springboard for everything that followed.
The website itself was engineered as a journey, not a catalog. The landing page opens with a full-screen video showcasing breathtaking scenes from across China—misty mountains, ancient temples, vibrant street markets, and modern cityscapes. As visitors scroll, they're guided through carefully curated stories that answer the questions every traveler asks: What will I experience? Who will guide me? Why should I trust you with my journey? Each section flows seamlessly into the next, building anticipation and desire. The migration from Shopify to a headless CMS gave the Travel to Qin team unprecedented flexibility to update content, share customer stories, and adapt their messaging in real-time.
The impact was immediate and profound. Shareholders praised the transformation, recognizing how the new platform elevated their entire brand positioning. The design system's success led to its adoption for the company's mobile app, ensuring travelers experience the same immersive storytelling across every touchpoint. Travel to Qin had evolved from a transactional booking site into a digital gateway that makes the dream of visiting China feel not just possible, but irresistible.

Dēpology

data-driven conversion optimization for a fast-growing Korean skincare brand's mobile ecommerce experience.
00

problem
Depology, a rapidly growing Korean skincare brand with a strong Instagram presence in the US market, was preparing to launch multiple new products for shadow testing through paid advertising. They needed optimized product landing pages to maximize conversion rates in their ad-to-web customer journey. However, initial analysis revealed critical friction points: heatmap data showed users struggling with the mobile product buybox, while analytics indicated high drop-off rates and shortened session lengths tied to confusing header navigation on mobile devices. These UX issues were directly hindering conversion potential at a crucial growth moment.

solution
A data-driven redesign focused on the two highest-impact friction points: the mobile buybox and header navigation. After conducting thorough analysis of heatmaps, conversion funnels, and session behavior, new designs were proposed and validated through A/B testing. The redesigned mobile buybox streamlined the purchase flow with clearer CTAs and improved information hierarchy, while the simplified header navigation reduced cognitive load and improved discoverability. Following successful test runs and stakeholder presentations that secured buy-in from both product and marketing leadership, the changes were implemented across new product landing pages and ultimately adopted site-wide.

Depology was at an inflection point. The Korean skincare brand had built a devoted following on Instagram and was ready to scale through aggressive product launches and paid advertising. But there was a problem: their existing product pages weren't converting at the rates needed to justify increased ad spend. With multiple new products queued for shadow testing—a strategy where products are launched quietly to gauge market response before full rollout—the team needed landing pages that could turn Instagram traffic into customers efficiently.
The first step was diagnosis. Heatmap analysis revealed that mobile users were tapping repeatedly on elements within the product buybox, suggesting confusion about how to add items to cart or select variants. Conversion funnel data confirmed the suspicion: a significant percentage of users were abandoning their sessions at the product page stage. Session length metrics told another story—users who did engage were spending less time on site than expected, often bouncing after encountering the cluttered header navigation. These weren't assumptions; they were patterns backed by behavioral data.
Armed with these insights, the redesign focused on surgical improvements rather than wholesale changes. The mobile buybox was reimagined with a clearer visual hierarchy: variant selectors were made more prominent, the add-to-cart button was enlarged and repositioned for thumb-friendly access, and product information was reorganized to answer questions in the order customers naturally asked them. The header navigation was simplified dramatically, reducing menu options and improving the information architecture to help users find what they needed without overwhelming them. Every decision was testable, measurable, and directly tied to observed user behavior.
The validation process was methodical. Test runs were launched on select product pages, with conversion rates monitored daily. The results were striking: the new designs drove a conversion rate increase that validated every hypothesis. With data in hand, the pitch to product and marketing leadership was straightforward—these changes weren't subjective design preferences, they were proven performance improvements. The stakeholders backed the rollout, and the new UI was implemented first across all new product landing pages, then gradually adopted across the entire site. Depology's mobile experience had transformed from a conversion bottleneck into a growth engine.

TUTU FUTURE

a strategic rebrand for a Chinese study tour operator expanding beyond hiking trips into academic and cultural programs.
00

problem
TUTU FUTURE, a study tour operator specializing in short-term European programs for Chinese students, had outgrown its original brand identity. The existing logo and visual system were built around a student hiking theme with green color palette—appropriate for their early adventure-focused tours but increasingly misaligned as they expanded into academic courses, art programs, STEM workshops, and language immersion experiences. The hiking-centric brand failed to communicate the breadth and sophistication of their evolving offerings, limiting their appeal to prospective students seeking diverse educational experiences in Europe.

solution
A comprehensive brand refresh that repositioned TUTU FUTURE from an outdoor adventure operator to a multifaceted educational travel platform. The new visual identity system included a modernized logo that balanced academic credibility with youthful energy, an expanded color palette that could flex across different program types (art, STEM, language, culture), and a complete suite of marketing materials optimized for social media campaigns. A custom mascot design added approachability and personality, while the new tone of voice guidelines ensured consistent communication across all touchpoints. The scalable system empowered the team to create cohesive materials for any program type without redesigning from scratch.

TUTU FUTURE's challenge was one of evolution. They had built a loyal following among Chinese students seeking European adventure, but their ambitions had grown beyond hiking trails. As they added academic programs, art workshops, STEM courses, and language immersion experiences to their portfolio, their green-and-outdoorsy brand identity began to feel like a limitation rather than an asset. Prospective students interested in studying Renaissance art in Florence or learning German in Berlin couldn't see themselves in a brand that screamed "backpacking trip."
The rebrand began with strategic positioning. Rather than abandoning their adventurous roots entirely, the goal was to elevate the concept of exploration—from physical journeys to intellectual and cultural discovery. The new logo retained a sense of movement and energy but shed the literal hiking references. Clean, modern typography conveyed academic credibility, while dynamic shapes suggested possibility and growth. The bilingual lockup (TUTU FUTURE 徒途学旅) honored their Chinese student audience while signaling their international scope.
Color became a critical tool for differentiation. The old single-green palette was replaced with a flexible system: vibrant blues for language programs, warm terracottas for art and culture, energetic purples for STEM, and yes, a refined green for outdoor experiences. Each program type could have its own visual personality while remaining unmistakably TUTU FUTURE. The mascot—a friendly, curious character—added warmth and approachability, giving the brand a face that could appear across social media, brochures, and promotional materials without feeling corporate or distant.
The impact was immediate. The new brand system gave TUTU FUTURE the visual language to market their full range of programs with confidence. Social media campaigns could now speak to art students, language learners, and STEM enthusiasts with equal authenticity. Printed brochures and digital materials maintained a cohesive look while celebrating the diversity of experiences on offer. The brand had grown up without losing its spirit, ready to guide the next generation of Chinese students on journeys that would shape their futures.

Stair Aid

an assistive mobility device that enables faster, safer stair navigation for people with temporary lower-limb injuries.
00

problem
People recovering from lower-limb injuries face a critical mobility challenge: standard crutches force them to navigate stairs slowly and awkwardly, creating safety risks and limiting independence. Traditional crutches require users to adjust length manually—a process that takes 30+ seconds per adjustment and demands both hands. For someone with an injured foot trying to climb stairs, this means choosing between using crutches at the wrong length (unsafe) or stopping to adjust them mid-staircase (impractical). The result is slow, unstable stair navigation that turns a simple task into a frustrating ordeal.

solution
StairAid is a reimagined crutch system designed specifically for stair navigation. The core innovation is a one-handed, trigger-activated length adjustment mechanism that allows users to extend or shorten each crutch in just 3 seconds—8 seconds for a pair. By positioning crutches at uneven lengths (front-back stance), users gain the stability needed to ascend and descend stairs quickly and safely. The curved tip provides a secure stepping point during adjustment and improves balance through increased contact area. A forearm brace replaces the traditional armpit support, reducing fatigue and improving agility on stairs.

The challenge began with a simple observation: people on crutches navigate stairs in a fundamentally different way than they walk on flat ground. On level surfaces, crutches are positioned symmetrically at the same length. But on stairs, the optimal stance is diagonal—one crutch higher, one lower—to create a stable tripod with the uninjured leg. Traditional crutches can't adapt to this need without a time-consuming manual adjustment, forcing users to compromise safety for convenience.
The design process started with experimentation. Using standard crutches, real-world stair tests revealed that uneven crutch lengths reduced stair descent time from 8 seconds to 6 seconds and improved stability dramatically. The insight was clear: if users could adjust crutch length instantly, they could optimize for both stair navigation and ground walking without friction. This became the core design objective—develop a length adjustment mechanism that works in seconds, not minutes.
The technical solution evolved through multiple iterations. Early prototypes used an external locking system mounted around the crutch tube, but user feedback revealed aesthetic concerns and mechanical complexity. The second iteration moved the mechanism inside the tube, making it invisible from the outside while reducing part count and improving durability. The locking system uses a lever for linear motion input, a bike cable for motion transfer, and a wedge mechanism to convert vertical hand motion into horizontal locking force. An extension spring keeps the length locked by default, requiring only a trigger pull to release.
Beyond the adjustment mechanism, every detail was optimized for stair use. The curved tip serves dual purposes: it provides a concave surface for applying force during length adjustment and creates a stable stepping point when users need to tilt the crutch on stairs. The forearm brace improves agility compared to traditional armpit supports, allowing users to shift weight dynamically. Material choices—Nylon-66 30-GF for structural components—ensure the design could withstand real-world loads while remaining lightweight. The result is a mobility aid that doesn't just accommodate stair navigation—it's designed for it.
